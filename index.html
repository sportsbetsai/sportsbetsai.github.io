Our Data

How it works

Scraping

We generally cover sports with public APIs or scrape-able datastores for two important data sources: (1) statistics and reference data, and (2) historical betting odds. (1) guarantees that we have a rich list of base signals to train our models. (2) allows us to backtest the performance of our models. We’ve built custom scrapers to interact with these APIs or crawl datastores for each sport market that we cover. These scrapers run nightly to pick up new data.

Magic Data

Bookmakers will make odds based on recent ‘feelings’ about players and teams. We make a good attempt to data-ify these feelings via a further step of data collection, where we aggregate data across multiple news, social media, and sports blogging platforms. These data are then transformed into numeric scores or enumeration levels and fed into our model.

Machine Learning

After a fair amount of cleaning and feature engineering, we transform our raw data into meaningful signals. In general, for each sport we will have between 100 – 500 such signals. We then train logistic models to predict the win percentages per game. The great majority of our models are neural networks, but occasionally we have also used boosted gradient decision trees. At this stage we tune some model hyperparameters for optimal performance.

Backtest & Trade

Once we have our predicted odds, we can compare them with odds from various bookmakers, and we make trades on games with highest differences. We have backtests of model performance over the course of the most recent 12 months. We do not optimise our model to backtest performance nor do we train over this period of data. We also have statistics on our real trading revenue; if interested, please contact us directly for that.
